T. G. Edwards - Music of the 55 Cancri Binary Star System for Cello & Modular Synthesizer

Over the past two years, TG has been exploring using distances between exoplanets as pitch material for new tuning systems, inspired by the ancient concept of musica universalis or “music of the spheres” interpreting the movement of celestial bodies (traditionally those within our own solar system) as music.

This composition is based on 55 Cancri- a star system 41 light years away, within the constellation of cancer. The star system features five planets- Galileo, Brahe, Lipperhey, Janssen and Harriot with varying properties such as Janssen’s oceans of lava and Harriot- a gas giant with potentially habitable moons. Featuring cello by Liz from Dead Space Chamber Music.

Part 1 features frequencies derived from the distances between the planets, so the lowest note representing the lava planet Janssen which is closest to the system's two stars.

About the event

Dark Alchemy Bristol is an independent event series promoting dark, experimental artists, both local and international. Established in 2017, Dark Alchemy is co-curated by Ellen Southern and Tom Bush (Dead Space Chamber Music) and Tommy Edwards. Our core events are held in unique spaces including crypts and churches, immersing audiences in the acoustics, aesthetic and atmosphere of each.

The Dark Alchemy event series has developed symbiotically with The Churches Conservation Trust- the country's pre-eminent trust for the management and repair of historic church properties, with more than 350 Grade 1 listed treasures in their care. The events raise awareness and funds to support the work of protecting and maintaining so many sacred spaces across the country, including St Thomas the Martyr.
