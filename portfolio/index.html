Music

20 minimal modular synthesizer explorations inspired by parapsychology journals from the late 19th century to the 1960s. The release follows a loose narrative about a fictional government department carrying out experiments into the supernatural. This is represented by experimental noise and drone pieces with occasional melodic passages to convey the emotions of the characters. Musically, the release is inspired by early electronic music such as the BBC Radiophonic Workshop, FC Judd and Peter Zinovieff; and early Tangerine Dream.

Live Footage

My live performances follow a similar structure to the above release in the form of noisey, experimental pieces, flowing into more melodic moments. I perform knelt on the floor with my own sparse lighting rather than the venue's own, to create a ritualistic scene.

Experiments

I enjoy making music with modular due to the tactile, interactive nature of the controls, the endless possibilities for experimentation and the temporary nature of the patches. In the above video, I experiment using a module with a heating element for burning incense; a module that detects changes in light; and a speech synthesizer among others, creating patches that allow the different elements to feed off of each other.


Other Current Projects

Black Panels Only is a print zine about Eurorack-format modular synthesizers. The zine features interviews with companies that produce modules and artists that use them. Aesthetically the zine draws from 80s and 90s zine culture and leans towards the dark and esoteric. Currently working on the seventh issue.


Dark Alchemy is a series of events that I co-organise with Ellen Southern and Tom Holmes, putting on experimental, atmospheric music in various venues in Bristol but most frequently in historic spaces such as the Crypt of St John-on-the-Wall and the Church of St Thomas the Martyr.
